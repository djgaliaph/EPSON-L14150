Troubleshooting

For computers
If security software notifies you that communication to EpsonNet Setup is blocked, you need to allow communication.
Change the settings for the security software to allow communication with EpsonNet Setup through the firewall.
For Wi-Fi
Turn the wireless router’s power off and on again. Consider other users who may be using the network.
Click Details, and then print a network connection report. Check the problems and solutions.
Depending on the diagnostic results, perform corrective actions using one of the following links.
For [PASS]
For [FAIL]
When you connect a device to the USB 3.0 port on a Mac, radio frequency interference may occur. Try the following if you cannot connect to wireless LAN (Wi-Fi) or if operations become unstable.
Place the device that is connected to the USB 3.0 port further away from the computer.
If the printer supports the 5 GHz frequency range, connect to the SSID for the 5 GHz range.
For Wired LAN
Turn the hub’s power off and on again. Consider other users who may be using the network. Check the connection again after approximately one minute from turning the hub’s power off and on again.
Check that the Ethernet cable is connected firmly. If the connection status light is off, check the light status by connecting to another port or changing the Ethernet cable. If the problem is not resolved, replace the hub.
